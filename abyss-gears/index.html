What Are Abyss Gears?

Abyss Gears are socketable skill gems you insert into weapons and armor. Unlike traditional RPG gems that just add stats, Abyss Gears grant actual combat abilities — special attacks, elemental effects, and passive bonuses. A "Flame Imbue" Gear socketed into a sword adds fire damage to all attacks. A "Frost Nova" Gear triggers an ice explosion on perfect dodge.

They are the key to endgame build customization. While your weapon determines base damage and your armor determines defense, Abyss Gears define how you fight. Two players with identical equipment can have completely different combat styles based on their Gear loadout.

Abyss Gear FAQ

Q: How do I get Abyss Gears?
Abyss Gears drop from bosses, world exploration, and story milestones throughout Pywel. Higher-tier Gears are found in endgame regions — Demeniss and the Crimson Desert. Some unique Gears are tied to specific boss encounters.
Q: Can I remove a socketed Abyss Gear?
Yes. Abyss Gears can be freely socketed and unsocketed at any time outside of combat. There is no cost to swap Gears, so experiment with different combinations before boss fights.
Q: How many Abyss Gears can I equip?
Each weapon and armor piece has 1-2 Gear slots depending on rarity. Legendary items typically have 2 slots. A full set of equipment can hold 6-12 Gears total, allowing for deep build customization.
Q: Do Abyss Gears stack with passive skills?
Yes. Abyss Gear effects stack additively with passive skills from your character's skill tree. For example, a +10% crit rate from Insight Gears adds to your base crit and any crit bonuses from the skill tree.
Q: What's the best Abyss Gear setup?
It depends on your character and build. Kliff benefits from Insight + Swift. Oongka needs Vigor. Damiane relies on Composure. See the Abyss Gear Tier List → for ranked comparisons, or use the Build Calculator to test your setup.
Q: Are Abyss Gears character-locked?
Most Abyss Gears are universal, but some Elemental Gears may be restricted by character or weapon type. Always check the Gear's description before socketing.
