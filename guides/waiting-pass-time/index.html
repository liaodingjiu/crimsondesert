How to Wait & Pass Time in Crimson Desert

There's no universal "wait" button — you need a rest point. Here's exactly how to pass time using bonfires, beds, and quest prompts on PC, Xbox, and PS5, plus the cooldown rule and every fix for when it won't work.

⏰ 1. The Quick Answer

Find a bonfire, cooking pot, or bed → aim your camera directly at the fire/pot → hold the interact button → the Wait menu appears → choose 3, 6, or 12 hours → time advances. You cannot simply press a button anywhere — you must be at a valid rest point.

💡 Key distinction: Unlike Skyrim or The Witcher 3 where you can "meditate" or "wait" anywhere, Crimson Desert requires you to be physically at a bonfire, cooking station, or bed. Explore to find them, then remember their locations.

🔥 2. All Three Ways to Pass Time

Method 1 — Most Common

Bonfires & Cooking Pots

Scattered throughout the wilderness, settlements, and camps across Pywel. Approach the fire or cooking pot, aim your camera directly at it (the prompt is camera-sensitive — looking slightly away hides it), then hold the Wait button. This is the fastest and most reliable method — no bed required.

Method 2 — Best for Long Skips

Beds (Camp & Settlements)

Use your own camp bed or any unoccupied bed in towns. Interact to sleep, then choose your duration (3/6/12 hours). Sleeping may also restore health or apply a rested bonus. If you don't see the sleep prompt, the bed might be occupied or you may still be on cooldown from a recent rest.

Method 3 — Quest-Only

Automatic Quest Time Skip

When a quest requires a specific time of day (e.g. "meet contact at midnight"), an hourglass icon ⏳ may appear near the quest marker. Select it and the game automatically advances time to meet the quest requirement — no bonfire needed. This only appears for certain quests and cannot be triggered manually.

The 10-Hour Cooldown

You cannot spam the wait mechanic. After waiting or sleeping, a cooldown of roughly 10 in-game hours activates before you can wait again. If you see the message "Now is not the time to sleep" or the Wait prompt won't appear, the cooldown is still active. Continue playing, explore, or fight — the cooldown ticks down as time passes naturally.

💡 Smart use: Combine waiting with other activities. Wait 12 hours → complete a quest or clear a camp → by the time you're done, the cooldown has expired and you can wait again. Don't stand around doing nothing — the game expects you to engage with content between rests.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How do you wait in Crimson Desert?
Find a bonfire, cooking pot, or bed. Aim your camera directly at the fire/pot/bed. Hold CTRL (PC) / LB (Xbox) / L1 (PS5) until the Wait menu appears. Select 3, 6, or 12 hours to advance time. There is no universal wait button — you must be at a valid rest point.
Q: Can you skip time without sleeping?
Yes — bonfires and cooking pots let you pass time without sleeping. Approach any campfire or cooking station, aim your camera at it, and hold the Wait button. No bed required. Quest-based time skips (hourglass icon ⏳) also bypass sleeping entirely.
Q: Why can't I sleep or wait?
Check: (1) Cooldown — you must wait ~10 in-game hours between rests. (2) Camera angle — look directly at the fire or pot; the prompt is camera-sensitive. (3) Early game — resting may not unlock until a certain story point. (4) Active quest — check for an hourglass icon at the quest marker; manual waiting may be overridden.
Q: How do I wait at a bonfire on Xbox / PS5?
Approach a bonfire or cooking pot → aim your camera directly at the fire → hold LB (Xbox) or L1 (PS5) until the Wait menu appears → use D-pad or Left Stick to select the duration → press A (Xbox) or X (PS5) to confirm. The prompt is camera-sensitive — look directly at the fire, not the surrounding area.
Q: What is the wait cooldown in Crimson Desert?
After waiting or sleeping, you cannot wait again for approximately 10 in-game hours. This prevents spamming the time-skip mechanic. The message "Now is not the time to sleep" means the cooldown hasn't expired yet. Continue playing or exploring until it ticks down.
Q: Can you change time in Crimson Desert?
Yes, through three methods: (1) Bonfire/Cooking Pot — fastest, choose 3/6/12 hour skip. (2) Bed — sleep to advance time, may provide rested bonus. (3) Quest hourglass prompt — automatically sets time for quest requirements. You cannot manually set a specific hour — only skip by fixed 3/6/12-hour increments.
