Crimson Desert Fishing Guide

Everything you need to know about fishing — how to get a rod, master the mini-game on PC, Xbox & PS5, find the best spots, and fix every common problem. Plus: a complete fish species catalog and rod upgrade path.

🎣 1. How to Get a Fishing Rod

Before you can fish, you need a rod. There are two ways to get your first one — buy it early with pocket change, or get it free through a short quest.

💰

Method 1 — Buy It (Fastest)

Visit the Provisioner Shop in Hernand. It's located directly opposite the Smithy. Purchase a basic Fishing Rod for 15 Copper. This is pocket change even at the start of the game — you can afford it before your first quest.

📋

Method 2 — Quest (Free)

Talk to Annabella at the Hanan Saddlery in Hernand. She'll give you a fishing rod as a reward for completing her request: catch a Tench in the pool just north of the bridge. This takes a few minutes and nets you a free rod plus quest XP.

💡 Pro Tip: Do Annabella's quest even if you already bought a rod. The quest XP and early-game rewards are worth it, and Tench are easy to catch in the pool right next to her.

🎮 2. How to Fish — Step-by-Step Mechanics

The in-game fishing tutorial can be confusing. Here's exactly how the mini-game works, broken down into four clear phases. Master the "tension and reel" rhythm in Step 4 — that's where most players struggle.

1

Equip & Cast Your Line

Open your equipment radial wheel and select the fishing rod. Find open water — rivers, streams, or lakes. Hold the aim button to bring up the targeting reticle, aim away from rocks or terrain, then release to cast.

2

Wait for a Bite

Watch your bobber. Wait 15 seconds to 1 minute. You'll hear a distinct splash and see an orange bubble appear as your bobber gets pulled underwater. That's your signal.

3

Set the Hook

Immediately press the hook button the moment you see the orange bubble and hear the splash. There's a short timing window — miss it and the fish escapes. Do not spam the button; press once, on time.

4

Tire the Fish & Reel In

Pull your rod in the opposite direction of the fish's movement. If it swims right, pull left. If it stays still and splashes, pull straight back. Once the white splashes and bubbles completely stop, reel in fast. Stop reeling instantly if the fish starts thrashing again — repeat until caught.

🎯 Don't cast too far out. Keeping your bobber close to the bank means less reeling distance when the fish tires. Also, hitting rocks or terrain with your cast will snap the line instantly.

🖐️ 3. Hand Fishing — Skip the Mini-Game

If the rod mini-game feels tedious, or you just need basic fish for cooking ingredients, there's a built-in bypass: walk directly into shallow rivers or lakes and press the interact prompt to catch fish with your bare hands.

⚠️ 4. Common Problems & How to Fix Them

If something goes wrong while fishing, it's almost always one of these four issues. Don't waste time guessing — check the symptom and apply the fix.

🔴

Line snaps immediately on cast

You throw the line and it breaks before reaching the water.
Fix: You're hitting rocks or terrain. Re-aim so the reticle shows open water with no obstacles. Casting too close to the shore edge or into rocky areas will snap the line instantly.
🔴

Line breaks during the fight

You hooked a fish, but the line snapped mid-fight.
Fix: You're reeling while the fish is still splashing. The golden rule: no reeling during splashes. White bubbles and splashing water = fish is fighting = only pull direction, never reel. Reel only when the water is completely calm with zero bubbles.
🟡

Can't reel in — nothing happens

The fish stopped splashing but reeling doesn't pull it in.
Fix: This is the #1 complaint on Xbox. Three things to check: (1) Make sure all white bubbles have completely disappeared — even a few tiny bubbles mean the fish is still fighting. (2) On controller, rotate the Right Analog Stick clockwise in a full circle — partial rotations don't register. (3) On PC, scroll the mouse wheel continuously, not one click at a time.
🟡

Fish escapes before I can hook it

You see the splash and orange bubble but the fish gets away.
Fix: The hook window is short — roughly 1-2 seconds after the orange bubble appears. Keep your finger ready on the hook button while waiting for a bite. Don't wait to confirm visually; press the hook button as soon as you hear the splash sound. Audio cue is faster than visual confirmation.

🍳 8. Fishing & Cooking — Why It Matters

Fish isn't just for selling. Many cooking recipes require specific fish as ingredients, and cooked dishes provide temporary stat boosts that make a real difference in boss fights. If you're planning to tackle the harder content, fishing is part of your preparation loop.

Check the Cooking Database → for recipes that use fish ingredients. Hand fishing is the fastest way to stockpile common fish for bulk cooking.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How do I get a fishing rod in Crimson Desert?
Two ways: Buy one from the Provisioner Shop in Hernand (opposite the Smithy) for 15 Copper, or complete Annabella's quest at the Hanan Saddlery — she gives you a free rod for catching a Tench in the pool north of the bridge.
Q: Why won't my line reel in while fishing?
You're reeling while the fish is still fighting. Never reel during splashes. Pull the rod opposite the fish's movement until all white bubbles and splashing completely stop. Only then reel in (Mouse Wheel on PC, Rotate Right Stick clockwise on controller). Reeling during the splash phase snaps your line.
Q: Can I fish without a rod?
Yes — hand fishing lets you catch basic fish by walking into shallow water and pressing Square (PS5) / X (Xbox) / the on-screen prompt (PC). This bypasses the mini-game entirely. Hand fishing is great for cooking ingredients but won't work for rare or legendary fish.
Q: How do I fish on PC with keyboard and mouse?
F2 to open equipment wheel → select rod → Q to aim → click/release Q to cast → Right-Click to hook when bobber dips → WASD to pull opposite the fish's direction → Scroll Mouse Wheel to reel when water is calm.
Q: How do I fish on Xbox / PS5 controller?
Hold Left D-pad to open radial → LT / L2 to aim → release to cast → RT / R2 to hook → Left Stick to fight direction → Rotate Right Stick clockwise to reel when water is calm. The reeling input requires full circle rotations — partial turns may not register.
Q: Where is the best fishing spot for beginners?
The pool north of the Hernand bridge is the ideal starter spot. NPCs fish there constantly — a reliable indicator of healthy fish populations. It has Tench for Annabella's quest, and the nearby shallows also allow hand fishing for quick ingredient runs.
