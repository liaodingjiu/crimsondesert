Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the best beginner class?
Kliff is the most beginner-friendly character due to his balanced stats, versatile weapon options (1H/2H swords, axes, daggers), and forgiving defense. He can tank hits while learning enemy patterns. Oongka is also a solid choice if you prefer a more aggressive spear-based playstyle, while Damiane requires more careful positioning as a ranged character.
Q: How long does it take to beat Crimson Desert?
The main story takes approximately 30–40 hours for a focused playthrough. Completing all side quests, optional bosses, and exploration adds another 20–30 hours. Full completion — including all Abyss Artifact unlocks, legendary gear farming, and all mount types — can exceed 80+ hours.
Q: Is Crimson Desert multiplayer?
Crimson Desert is primarily a single-player open-world action RPG. There is no co-op campaign or persistent multiplayer. However, Pearl Abyss has indicated that post-launch content may include optional online features such as leaderboards and asynchronous social elements. The core experience is solo.
Q: What is the hardest boss in the game?
The Crimson Dragon (endgame flying boss) is widely considered the hardest encounter. It requires mastery of aerial combat on dragonback, has multiple phase transitions with devastating AOE attacks, and demands optimized gear (Tier 5 legendary weapons, full Abyss Gear set). Other challenging fights include Goyen (mid-game skill check) and the Abyss Warden (mechanical complexity).
