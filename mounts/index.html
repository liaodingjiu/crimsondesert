Mounted Combat Mechanics

Mounts in Crimson Desert are more than just transportation — they are integral to combat. While mounted, Kliff can use two-handed weapons with increased reach, Oongka can charge with spears for devastating impact, and Damiane can fire arrows with enhanced mobility. Each mount type offers different combat bonuses:

  • Horses — +15% movement speed during combat, lighter attack animations
  • Bears — +40% stagger resistance, roar AOE that debuffs nearby enemies
  • Dragons — Full aerial combat with fire breath, dive attacks, and immunity to ground AOE
  • Special Mounts — Unique combat traits depending on the mount variant

How to Obtain Mounts

Mounts are acquired through several methods. Horses can be tamed in the wild using a Lasso (crafted at any stable). Bears require completing the "Beast Master" questline in the Hernand region. Dragons are locked behind the Crimson Desert main story arc. Special mounts are tied to specific NPC quests, faction reputation rewards, or seasonal events. Once unlocked, mounts are summoned via the Whistle item in your quick-slot.

Mount FAQ

Q: How do I get my first mount?
Your first horse becomes available early in Act 1 after completing the tutorial. Visit any stable in Hernand or Demeniss and speak to the stablemaster. You can either purchase a basic horse for gold or tame a wild horse in the open fields using a Lasso. Wild-tamed horses often have slightly better starting stats than purchased ones.
Q: Can you fight while mounted?
Yes! Crimson Desert features full mounted combat. Each character has unique mounted movesets: Kliff performs sweeping two-handed strikes, Oongka executes devastating spear charges, and Damiane fires arrows with 360-degree mobility. Bears add melee AOE attacks, and dragons allow full aerial combat with dive attacks and breath weapons. Mounted combat drains mount stamina, so manage it carefully.
Q: What is the fastest mount?
Among standard mounts, the Crimson Steed (Legendary-tier horse) has the highest base speed. However, for sheer traversal efficiency, dragons are unmatched — they fly over all terrain and obstacles, making them functionally the fastest way to reach any destination. Special mounts with speed bonuses (such as the Windrunner variant) can rival dragons on flat ground.
Q: Are there flying mounts in Crimson Desert?
Yes — dragons are the game's flying mounts, unlocked in the endgame Crimson Desert region. Unlike ground mounts, dragons allow true three-dimensional flight, letting you soar above mountains, cross chasms, and engage flying enemies. Dragon flight has a stamina meter that depletes while airborne; land periodically to regenerate. Aerial combat with dragons is a core endgame mechanic.
